SWEET INDRAJAO


Sweet Indrajao (Wrightia tinctoria) is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to India and Burma with smooth, light gray bark. It is also known by other names, including Pala indigo plant, Dyer's oleander, Ivory tree, and Ester plant.

Uses

Sweet Indrajao has many uses, including:
- Skin
- The bark has astringent properties that can treat eczema, dermatitis, and psoriasis. Crushed fresh leaves can relieve toothaches.
- Wound healing
- Diabetes
- Weight loss
- Gastrointestinal problems
Sweet Indrajao can help with diarrhea, fever, abdominal pain, flatulence, bilious troubles, and sexual desire.
