Snap Ginger



Alpinia calcarata, also known as snap ginger, is a perennial herb that is native to India and Sri Lanka. It is cultivated in tropical countries, including Malaysia and India, and is often used in traditional Sri Lankan medicine. The rhizomes of the plant are said to have antibacterial, antifungal, anthelmintic, antinociceptive, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, aphrodisiac, gastroprotective, and antidiabetic properties.
